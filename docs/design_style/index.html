ORIGINS & PHILOSOPHY

Cyberpunk is a subgenre of science fiction in a dystopian futuristic setting that tends to focus on a "combination of low-life and high tech". It features advanced technological and scientific achievements, such as artificial intelligence and cybernetics, juxtaposed with a degree of breakdown or radical change in the social order.

Visually, it is defined by neon lights, dark cityscapes, Japanese typography, and a clash between the organic and the synthetic.

The Golden Age of Excess

As seen in this visual capture, the Kitsch style dominates with its unapologetic use of neon gradients and rounded, approachable geometry. It prioritizes "Style over Substance," using vibrant pinks and cyans to overwhelm the senses and mask the dystopian reality.

  • Rounded Edges & Soft UI
  • High-Saturation Neon Palette
  • Form-First, Function-Second
  • Pop-Culture & Street Level Aesthetics

STYLE: NEO-MILITARISM

SUBSTANCE OVER STYLE

Corporate Authority

This visual archive demonstrates the cold efficiency of Neo-Militarism. Defined by "Substance over Style," it utilizes sharp, angular geometry and a muted, tactical color palette to convey absolute power, corporate dominance, and lethal functionality.

  • Sharp, Aggressive Angles
  • Muted Tactical Tones (Gunmetal, Red)
  • Function-First, Intimidating Design
  • Corporate & Military Precision
